Cardiovascular or Aerobic Fitness

Cardiovascular fitness targets the muscles’ ability to make the best use of oxygen so that they can produce energy for movement.

Anaerobic Fitness

  • Weight-lifting,
  • Sprinting,
  • Plyometrics (movements associated with increased muscular power),
  • Isometrics (activity done from a static position), and
  • High-intensity interval training which requires repetitive short and powerful movements.

Muscular Strength and Endurance

Muscular strength is a type of fitness directly related to muscle density and endurance, allowing athletes to perform more repetitions while lifting heavier weights. It increases a person’s ability to work against resistance by maximising the force that can be applied against a load.
